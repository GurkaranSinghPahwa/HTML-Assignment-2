Biography

Guru Gobind Singh Ji , the Tenth Guru of Sikhs was born on January 5, 1667 in Patna Sahib, Bihar, India. He was the last human Guru of the Sikhs. He became Guru on November 24, 1675 at the age of nine, following the martyrdom of his father, the ninth Guru, Guru Tegh Bahadur Ji. He was a Divine messenger, a warrior, a poet, a philosopher and he is the person who molded the Sikh religion into its present shape, with the institution of the Khalsa fraternity, and the completion of the sacred scripture, Guru Granth Sahib Ji . He sacrificed his whole family for the sake of humanity.

1 Battle of Bhangani September 1688 Battle of Bhangani was the first battle fought by Guru Gobind Singh with hill Chieftains. Guru Gobind Singh ji was 20 of age when he fought this battle. Guru Gobind Singh fought the Battle of Bhangani against an allied force of Bhim Chand, Garwal king Fateh Khan and other local kings of the Sivalik Hills. The battle lasted for a day and thousands of lives were lost. The Guru came out victorious.
